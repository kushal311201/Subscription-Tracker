Local Data Storage

Subscription Tracker uses your device's local storage to save your subscription data. This means all your data stays on your device and is not sent to any external servers.

Getting Started

Welcome to Subscription Manager! This guide will help you set up and use the app effectively.

Data Storage Information

1
Local Storage

Your subscription data is stored locally on your device using IndexedDB, a powerful browser database.

No account creation is needed - just add your subscriptions and they'll be saved on your current device.

2
Privacy First

We prioritize your privacy. Your subscription data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to export or sync it.

3
Installing the App

For the best experience, you can install this as a Progressive Web App (PWA):

  • In Chrome/Edge: Look for the install icon in the address bar
  • In Safari (iOS): Tap the Share button, then "Add to Home Screen"

Installing as a PWA allows the app to work offline and provides a more app-like experience.

Using the App

Adding a Subscription

1

Fill out the "Add New Subscription" form with your subscription details.

All fields are required.

Click "Add Subscription" to save it.

Viewing Subscriptions

1

All your subscriptions appear as cards in the "Your Subscriptions" section.

Cards show details like name, amount, due date, and days until renewal.

Cards are sorted by due date (soonest first).

Subscriptions due within 3 days will show a red warning.

Filtering & Searching

1

Use the category dropdown to filter by subscription type.

Use the search box to find subscriptions by name.

Editing & Deleting

1

Click the edit icon on a subscription card to edit its details.

The form will be filled with the subscription's current information.

Make your changes and click "Update Subscription".

To delete a subscription, click the trash icon and confirm.

Monthly Cost & Chart

1

The "Monthly Burn Rate" shows your total monthly subscription costs.

The pie chart visualizes spending by category.

Hover over chart segments to see detailed amounts and percentages.

Data Management

Exporting Your Data

1

To export your subscription data:

  1. Open the Settings panel
  2. Click on "Export Data"
  3. Save the JSON file to your device

This file contains all your subscription information and can be used to backup or transfer your data.

Importing Data

1

To import subscription data from a previous export:

  1. Open the Settings panel
  2. Click on "Import Data"
  3. Select your previously exported JSON file
  4. Confirm the import when prompted

Resetting Data

1

To reset all your data:

  1. Open the Settings panel
  2. Click on "Reset Data"
  3. Confirm the reset when prompted

Warning: This will permanently delete all your subscription data from the app. Consider exporting your data first if you might need it later.

Troubleshooting

App Not Loading Subscriptions

Try refreshing the page. If that doesn't work, try clearing your browser cache.

If you're in private/incognito browsing mode, the app may have limited storage capabilities.

Can't Add or Edit Subscriptions

Make sure you're not in private/incognito browsing mode.

Check if your browser supports IndexedDB (all modern browsers do).

Try clearing some browser storage if your device is low on space.

Chart Not Displaying

Make sure you have at least one subscription added.

Try refreshing the page.

Check if JavaScript is enabled in your browser.
